When were eggs finally noticed?

Around 1400 BC, the Egyptians and the Chinese were starting the realize the potential that the egg had.
The started by hunting fowls, quails, and many other forms of duck, they would save the eggs but rather
than eat them, they would leave them to hatch to restock the wildlife population.

Eggs significance in history

Contrary to popular belief, omelettes didn't originate from france but rather from ancient Rome. Beaten
eggs were placed in a pan over a fire, then were rolled or folded, sweetened with honey and was served hot
This dish was called 'overmele' which meant 'eggs and honey' in their language. Omelettes were lost to the
world between 476 BC and around 1500 BC, when a man in france reinvented the dish altogether, using savoury
ingredients rather than sweetened.
